2. One factor I believe is important to consider when discussing the legality of web scraping is what
   information is being scraped. If the information is not anything including private personal information
   then I don't think it is as concerning as if it is private personal information. For security reasons, 
   having private personal information scraped from the web is very scary. However, scraping the web for
   something like ice cream flavors wouldn't pose any real threat.

3. The first guideline I think is important to consider when deciding whether to do web scraping is can
   the information be collected through a voluntary survey. When it comes to collecting personal information,
   the people who's information is being collected should be able to decided whether or not they want it to
   be collected, making a survey a more ethical choice. The second guideline I think is important to consider
   when deciding whether to do web scraping is what are the terms and conditions of the place the information
   is being scraped from. If a website asks users to consent to their information being shared before they
   use the site, then scraping information from that site is more ethical than scraping information from a
   site that does not ask for users' consent to their information being shared.
